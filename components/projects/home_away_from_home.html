Home away from Home

Overview

International students face unique challenges when securing on-campus housing, including high costs, cultural mismatches, and inefficient application systems. This UX research project focused on identifying these systemic barriers and proposing solutions to improve housing accessibility. My role involved conducting surveys, interviews, and competitive analysis to provide actionable recommendations.

Research and Planning

To uncover key insights, I utilized a mix of qualitative and quantitative methods:

  1. Surveys:

    • Distributed surveys to international students to explore housing affordability, satisfaction, and preferred amenities.
    • Many respondents reported financial strain from seasonal rent hikes, especially during peak semesters.

  2. Interviews:

    • Conducted 8 in-depth interviews with international students to understand their cultural and logistical housing challenges.
    • Students emphasized the importance of community spaces, such as prayer rooms and culturally inclusive dining areas.
    • Key Quote: “I’d feel more at home if there were spaces where I could cook meals from my culture with others.”

  3. Competitive Analysis:

    • Benchmarked local housing providers and university programs, identifying successful practices like tiered pricing and roommate-matching systems.

Findings

Key insights included:

  1. Affordability Issues: Rent spikes during peak semesters made housing unaffordable for many students.
  2. Cultural Barriers: The lack of shared community spaces and roommate matching based on cultural compatibility created discomfort.
  3. Inefficient Application Systems: Most students found the housing application process slow and confusing, with limited transparency about room availability.

Recommendations

  1. Flexible Pricing: Implement tiered pricing models and provide off-season discounts to support students with limited budgets.
  2. Inclusive Spaces: Develop community kitchens, prayer rooms, and study lounges to enhance cultural inclusivity.
  3. Streamlined Application Systems: Create a centralized, multilingual platform with real-time room availability updates and transparent pricing.

Conclusion

This research provided actionable strategies to improve on-campus housing accessibility for international students. The findings underscored the importance of addressing affordability and inclusivity to create a welcoming environment. This experience strengthened my ability to address systemic challenges through user-centered research.
