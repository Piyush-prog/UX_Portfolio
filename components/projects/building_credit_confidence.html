Building Credit Confidence

Overview

Credit scores are a significant barrier to securing housing for many low-income renters and newcomers. This project aimed to create an educational platform that demystifies credit scores and provides actionable tools, including a credit simulator and personalized improvement plans. My role included conducting user research, prototyping, and iterative usability testing.

Research and Planning

The research phase was foundational to understanding user needs and defining the platform’s scope. A mixed-methods approach was employed:

  1. Stakeholder Interviews:

    • Conducted interviews with housing advocates and community leaders to understand gaps in financial literacy resources.
    • Advocates highlighted the lack of accessible tools that guide renters in building credit through everyday actions like rent payments.

  2. User Surveys:

    • Explored knowledge gaps and preferences for interactive financial tools.
    • Common feedback included frustration with confusing interfaces and an overwhelming amount of information on existing platforms.

  3. Competitive Analysis:

    • Reviewed existing tools like Credit Karma and Mint.
    • These platforms focused on credit monitoring but lacked localized, actionable guidance tailored to housing needs.

Design and Implementation

  1. Wireframes:

    • Developed prototypes featuring a credit simulator, educational modules, and personalized improvement plans.

  2. Usability Testing:

    • Conducted two rounds of testing, gathering feedback on navigation clarity, feature understanding, and accessibility.
    • Key changes included simplifying navigation, adding multilingual support, and improving progress tracking visuals.

Findings

  1. Knowledge Gaps: Users lacked a clear understanding of how specific actions, such as timely rent payments, influence credit scores.
  2. Accessibility Needs: Multilingual support and simplified text formatting were essential for inclusivity.
  3. Progress Tracking: Users appreciated tools that visualized their improvement over time and provided actionable next steps.

Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge: Difficulty in balancing educational depth with simplicity.

    Solution: Modularized content into easy-to-digest sections while offering optional in-depth resources.

  • Challenge: Users were overwhelmed by the interface during initial testing.

    Solution: Introduced color-coded navigation tabs and added interactive tooltips for first-time users.

Conclusion

The platform bridged critical knowledge gaps, empowering users to improve their credit scores and housing opportunities. This project underscored the value of combining education with interactivity and accessibility. It also reinforced my commitment to creating tools that address systemic challenges in underserved communities.
